Core 5 Meeting Update

Customer APAP Adoption Funnel

  • Deployment & Setup - Agency receives VR hardware prior to their CEW equipment, and their PSO services prior to their first CEW training. PSM logs the CEW training date to trigger timely CSM engagement.
  • Initial Onboarding Support - CSM outreach is triggered via Call-To-Action in Gainsight 90 priors to their CEW training start date to ensure the agency is ready to integrate VR into their initial CEW rollout.
  • Core Training Implementation - Agency deploys at least one Core Validation or Range Skill module plus the Gauntlet Validation Course as part of CEW certification for every officer. 
    • These specific modules are not a hard APAP requirement, just the 2x SIM trainings per person. These specific modules are the recommended implementation as a part of the version 24 TASER Training booklet.
  • Ongoing Adoption Support - CSM outreach is triggered via Call-To-Action in Gainsight 2 weeks after the CEW Training program begins to ensure the agency was successful. Another Call-To-Action is triggered 6 months later to facilitate VR is incorporated into next year’s annual training program.
  • Ongoing Micro-Training Adoption - Agency incorporates CET, vRBT-LA, or Verbal Skills (launching Fall 2025) to deliver 36+ VR trainings every 6 months across the agency.
Our core business priority is ensuring Steps 1–4 are executed consistently across new T10-VR customers. Step 5 represents advanced adoption, typically seen in more mature / larger agencies, mainly because these modules are beyond the scope of annual TASER certification programs. We are developing a playbook to guide customers to achieve these advanced forms of adoption (aka micro-training and content expansion) for 2026 and beyond.

Gains are driven by 15 new agencies meeting threshold, including 5 Majors. Non-SIM adoption (CET, vRBT-LA) is not being prioritized and will remain the core blocker to reaching APAP goals; under a SIM-only threshold, performance would be 15.5%.  When only looking at TASER 10 agencies with a SIM-Only threshold, performance would be 17.6% (+2.1 ppts from T10-T7 population).  If we adjusted the threshold down to 1 scenario per officer (instead of 1.5) for T10 and SIM only, performance would be 27.3% (+2.6 ppts from T10-T7 population).

While we had initially considered including VR in CSMs’ 2H APAP compensation plans, we collectively agreed to de-prioritize VR APAP as a focus area given the limited near-term impact of driving non-SIM usage. As a result, CSMs are currently focused only on our T10-VR M&M efforts, which center on onboarding new Major Accounts.

Product and CSM leadership agree that the M&M onboarding efforts are worth expanding to all line sizes given they help create a strong foundation for long-term adoption. We are exploring a SPIFF to align incentives around this onboarding focus, which CSM leadership will propose to executive leadership on 8/19. Regardless of funding, we plan to expand our onboarding workflows across all line sizes to support this shift in execution. 

As for the T10-VR Major Accounts M&M, we are still trending Green to reaching our end of year goal of at least 75% of new accounts using VR in their initial CEW rollout. We are tracking 14 new T10-VR Major Accounts since 4/1/25,  including 3 Corrections Agencies. This past month we also had our first two agencies -- Arizona DOC and Sacramento County SO — complete their PSO deployment and both agencies are on track to integrate VR into their upcoming CEW trainings. Reporting on new, eligible T10 Major Accounts is available here.

Top Adoption Blockers
  • Low non-SIM (CET + vRBT-LA) Adoption amongst agencies who are already meeting our SIM threshold (185 agencies representing 12,460 points / 7.1% Points)
  • Our SIM threshold is more difficult for our 89 Major Accounts to achieve, who represent over 60% of our possible points. (24 agencies have over 500+ SIM trainings in the last year, representing over 30k points / 18% Points)
  • CSMs do not have visibility into VR APAP / Product telemetry in their APAP dashboards, and therefore are not aware of who to target to increase APAP totals. (Note we have periodically been providing the CSM team lists of high-priority customers that we manually identified on their behalf).

Lead Hypothesis 

Major Agencies continue to drive a disproportionate share of VR engagement: while they represent fewer than 10% of all customers (89 agencies), they account for over 60% of all recorded training sessions in 2025. These agencies typically maintain steady monthly usage, especially those engaged with more than one content type. Baltimore City PD is the clearest example of year-round, multi-program training.

Despite being among our top 10 adopters, five agencies—including our highest-performing customer, Baltimore City PD—are not meeting the current APAP threshold. This misalignment stems from two factors: (1) Major Accounts take longer to reach the 1.5x SIM completion threshold due to their scale, and (2) Major Accounts are 3x more likely to use non-SIM products, which are not weighted equally in our thresholds. As Major Accounts represent 60% of possible APAP points, we will incorporate these insights into the 2026 APAP framework to better reflect adoption trends within this segment.

Key Themes and Insights

This Month’s APAP Gains Driven by Newly Eligible Agencies


Nearly all of this month’s APAP growth comes from newly eligible agencies meeting threshold in their first month, rather than improvements from previously active accounts. Of the 15 new adopters, 12 have had the product for less than a year, and 10 met threshold immediately upon eligibility — a strong signal that these agencies likely integrated VR into their CEW training programs right at deployment.

Notably, 4 of this month’s new adopters were Major Agencies — a surprising trend given the typical rollout challenges they face. Upon a deeper analysis, we learned that while smaller agencies and mid-size agencies are more likely to meet APAP than Majors (3x and 1.5x respectively), Majors who do adopt tend to do so faster — 5% faster than smaller agencies and 17% faster than mid-size agencies. Adoption is also trending 5% faster among customers who purchased within the past year.

These encouraging trends likely reflect both the increased emphasis on VR in Version 24 CEW training and our targeted onboarding improvements for Major Agencies — which include enhanced PSO services and more effective CSM handoffs that support early integration of VR into CEW rollouts.


SIM Adoption Sets Another All-Time High in July


July marked the sixth time in 2025 that Simulator Training set a new single-month record, with 20,302 total completions— a +16% MoM increase. Usage expanded across 439 agencies, up 22% MoM, now representing 54% of all eligible agencies.

Despite this broader reach, per-agency usage remained stable, averaging 46 trainings per agency, which is in line with the 90-day average and only slightly below June (-5% MoM). This indicates that new agencies are adopting the product at the same depth as our earlier-engaged users.

This growth is particularly notable given that July is typically a seasonal low for training — in fact, July 2024 saw 47% fewer trainings than the high set in May that year. This year-over-year reversal suggests a significant shift in how SIM is being integrated into agencies’ mid-year training cycles.


Top Issues, Blockers and Help Needed from Other Teams
  • Integrate VR Telemetry into CSM Dashboards - As of 6/30, VR product telemetry is live in Snowflake and ready for reporting via Sigma. However, integration into CSM dashboards is blocked due to capacity constraints amongst the  Revenue Intelligence team who own the dashboards; no ETA is yet available. Until this is resolved, CSMs lack visibility into in-market adoption—limiting their ability to proactively support customers beyond onboarding new Major Accounts under the T10-VR M&M program.
Next Actions (3-month horizon)
  • Revamped Product Playbook for customer-facing teams. Our existing document is bloated beyond use as it attempts to make internal users product experts to be able to speak to customers effectively. Instead we are exploring ways to leverage a custom GPT to help educate users on the products we offer as well as offer talking points to help drive conversations with customers, especially as it relates to adoption. We are targeting 9/30 to have a first version available for a small pilot amongst CSMs.
  • Include VR in the CSM APAP Dashboard - We are partnering with Connor Stirling to incorporate VR into CSM APAP Dashboards so that CSMs can have better visibility into their agencies’ VR APAP status. While VR is not currently a part of CSM APAP compensation incentives, we are pushing to incorporate this data into the dashboard by 9/30 in preparation for future initiatives (e.g., 2026 APAP).
  • Build Deeper Agency Adoption Reports in Sigma - In addition to integrating APAP metrics into the CSM APAP dashboard, we’re building a new Sigma dashboards over the next three months to give CSMs deeper visibility into agency adoption trends. The dashboard will provide a detailed view of an agency’s usage across all VR products over time — enabling CSMs to identify engagement patterns, spot drop-offs, and proactively prioritize outreach based on opportunity and risk. The dashboard is targeted to go live by end of year, in time to support 2026 APAP strategy and engagement planning.
Opportunities (currently not being prioritized in 3-month horizon and why)
  • We are not utilizing overrides for either agencies currently meeting our Shadow Goal (SIM-Only threshold), or for major accounts showing strong product adoption across the portfolio (ie., Charlotte PD and Baltimore City PD).
