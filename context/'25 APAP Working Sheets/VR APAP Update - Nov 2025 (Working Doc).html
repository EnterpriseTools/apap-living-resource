2New Agencies Require High-Touch Onboarding for Initial TASER Certification

Axon VR is a broad and complex solution that requires agencies to be properly trained—not just on the hardware and UI, but on how to design and deliver training programs using the right content. This is especially critical during onboarding, where early success is tied to how well the agency integrates VR into initial TASER certification.


Key Blockers:
1) PSM bandwidth is stretched while onsite — significant time is spent on device setup and updates, leaving less time to train agencies on effective usage.
2) Non-Major Accounts lack post-deployment support, as they don’t receive dedicated CSM engagement after PSO concludes.
3) Virtual PSO deployments underperform — agencies that go through a virtual onboarding complete only 16% as many trainings as those with physical onsites.

+ 1044 Agenices
+79,358 Points

3) Remove virtual PSO as a sellable service in 2026 pricebooks (approved in 2026 Pricing Strategy ✅)
New Agencies immediately meet core use case, priming them to expand VR further into their training programs.

3Recommended VR Trainings for T10 Re-Certification do not Satisfy Customer Needs

Nearly 40% of agencies experience a 50%+ drop in VR usage during TASER re-certification (Year 2+) compared to their initial certification year—indicating a misalignment between our VR offering and re-certification needs.


Key Blockers:
1) Validation Courses are too long (3 courses at ~20 mins each), making them impractical for re-cert blocks. Agencies like Lincoln PD have opted out as a result.
2) Version 24 training booklet excludes VR guidance for T7 agencies, which make up a large portion of re-certifying customers.
3) Range Skills content may not align well with re-certification goals (hypothesis).
4) Relevant re-cert content (e.g., vRBT scenarios) lacks visibility, especially for trainers not actively engaged post-deployment (hypothesis).

4Product UX Requires High Trainer Involvement, Limiting Training Throughput

The current VR experience places a high burden on trainers to set up, facilitate, and troubleshoot, reducing the number of users who can train independently—particularly in decentralized or downtime settings.


Key Blockers:
1) Users are not VR experts — common issues like incorrect content selection, unpaired controllers, or boundary prompts require real-time trainer intervention.
2) Hardware is difficult to manage at scale, especially for infrequent use—trainers face challenges with charging, updates, storage, and sanitation across multiple headsets, especially in de-centralized settings.

3) Conduct a study in 2026 that analyzes how many VR training sessions are required for proficiency with the solution, and how quickly that proficiency decays without continued use. (On Track - ATL as 2026 ZBB)

5Poor Onboarding Experience Limits Long-Term Product Engagement

Agencies that had a weak initial onboarding experience—particularly those now 2+ years into their contract—are unlikely to re-engage with the latest version of the VR product, despite major improvements.


Key Blockers:
1) Low initial CSAT due to early UX limitations (e.g., wrist trackers).
2) Minimal onboarding support, with no CSM follow-up post-deployment.
3) Low awareness of new content and features that may now better align with their current training needs.

Commentary (< 400 Words)

APAP declined 111bps MoM, primarily due to churn (-506 pts) and a net drag from new eligible agencies. While 28 new agencies met threshold, 17 churned, including three major point contributors (Metro Nashville PD, New Mexico DPS, Athens-Clarke County PD). Most churned agencies maintained SIM usage but failed on Non-SIM requirements. Additionally, 43 agencies became newly eligible, with 15 of those immediately meeting threshold but who were outweighed by the 28 agencies not yet adopting.

Despite the APAP drop, SIM adoption continues to accelerate, setting a new record with 23,895 trainings (+13% MoM) across 56% of eligible agencies (+6.7ppts). This marks the 8th record month for SIM in 2025, with overall VR usage up 66% YoY.

Major Agency adoption also grew, with:
7 new Majors meeting APAP (15 total, +3.1 ppts MoM)
7 additional Majors meeting the Shadow Goal (20 total, +3.1 ppts MoM)

This continued momentum reflects stronger VR-T10 training integration and targeted onboarding and follow-up from PSO and CSM teams.
