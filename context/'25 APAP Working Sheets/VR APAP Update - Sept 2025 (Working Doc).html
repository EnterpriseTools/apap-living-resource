2New Agencies Require High-Touch Onboarding for Initial TASER Certification

Axon VR is a broad and complex solution that requires agencies to be properly trained—not just on the hardware and UI, but on how to design and deliver training programs using the right content. This is especially critical during onboarding, where early success is tied to how well the agency integrates VR into initial TASER certification.


Key Blockers:
1) PSM bandwidth is stretched while onsite — significant time is spent on device setup and updates, leaving less time to train agencies on effective usage.
2) Non-Major Accounts lack post-deployment support, as they don’t receive dedicated CSM engagement after PSO concludes.
3) Virtual PSO deployments underperform — agencies that go through a virtual onboarding complete only 16% as many trainings as those with physical onsites.

+ 7.3 ppts

+ 284 Agenices
+18,094 Points
1) Scope LOE to provision devices in-house prior to shipping to customer for 2026 ZBB. (In Progress for ZBB)

2) Expand CSM onboarding workflows to T1200 net-new T10/VR agencies by 9/30. 10/10 (On Track - delay a result of EOQ3 TAP push by CSMs)

3) Remove virtual PSO as a sellable service in 2026 pricebooks (approved in 2026 Pricing Strategy). (Done ✅)
New Agencies immediately meet core use case, priming them to expand VR further into their training programs.

3Recommended VR Trainings for T10 Re-Certification do not Satisfy Customer Needs

Nearly 40% of agencies experience a 50%+ drop in VR usage during TASER re-certification (Year 2+) compared to their initial certification year—indicating a misalignment between our VR offering and re-certification needs.


Key Blockers:
1) Validation Courses are too long (3 courses at ~20 mins each), making them impractical for re-cert blocks. Agencies like Lincoln PD have opted out as a result.
2) Version 24 training booklet excludes VR guidance for T7 agencies, which make up a large portion of re-certifying customers.
3) Range Skills content may not align well with re-certification goals (hypothesis).
4) Relevant re-cert content (e.g., vRBT scenarios) lacks visibility, especially for trainers not actively engaged post-deployment (hypothesis).

4Product UX Requires High Trainer Involvement, Limiting Training Throughput

The current VR experience places a high burden on trainers to set up, facilitate, and troubleshoot, reducing the number of users who can train independently—particularly in decentralized or downtime settings.


Key Blockers:
1) Users are not VR experts — common issues like incorrect content selection, unpaired controllers, or boundary prompts require real-time trainer intervention.
2) Hardware is difficult to manage at scale, especially for infrequent use—trainers face challenges with charging, updates, storage, and sanitation across multiple headsets, especially in de-centralized settings.

+231 Agencies
+ 49,945 points

3) Conduct a study in 2026 that analyzes how many VR training sessions are required for proficiency with the solution, and how quickly that proficiency decays without continued use. (On Track - ATL in 2026 ZBB)
Agencies can do more trainings within the same amount of time dedicated for VR during their annual TASER Certifications.

5Poor Onboarding Experience Limits Long-Term Product Engagement

Agencies that had a weak initial onboarding experience—particularly those now 2+ years into their contract—are unlikely to re-engage with the latest version of the VR product, despite major improvements.


Key Blockers:
1) Low initial CSAT due to early UX limitations (e.g., wrist trackers).
2) Minimal onboarding support, with no CSM follow-up post-deployment.
3) Low awareness of new content and features that may now better align with their current training needs.

6Agencies Struggle to Expand VR Beyond TASER Training

As Axon VR expands into areas like Behavioral Health, Specialized Tactics, and Verbal Communication, most agencies are not adopting these non-TASER training modules, limiting the platform’s broader impact.


Key Blockers:
1) VR trainers are typically CEW instructors and are not positioned to champion other training integrations.
2) Trainers onboarded via PSO aren’t equipped to train others, limiting program scalability.
3) Low awareness of new content and lack of clear guidance on how to integrate it into broader training programs.

+13,811 Points

3) Release our Curriculum Builder GPT by 12/31 to either internal partners and/or to customers directly as a tool to build out training programs using various VR trainings. (Possibly Delayed - Awaiting guidance on broader Axon Assistant AI Tools)

Despite the drop in APAP MoM, SIM adoption continues to climb — September marked the highest month of use ever (21,148 trainings), up 16% MoM, with 49% of eligible agencies using SIM at an average of 46 completions per agency (+11%). Across all products, we see a +139% increase in trainings YoY.

Major Agency adoption also continues to grow, with four additional agencies now averaging 1+ SIM completions per person (15 total Major Agencies, +36.4% MoM). Notably, there was no increase in Major Agencies averaging 0.5+ completions per person, indicating that existing SIM users are increasing usage, but no new or previously inactive Majors engaged more than in prior months.

Looking back to February, we’ve seen strong growth not only in Shadow Goal attainment (+7.4 ppts) but also among agencies just below the threshold. 465 agencies are now averaging 1+ SIM completion per officer (+289 agencies since February), a group that accounts for 30.2% of total APAP points (+16.1 ppts from February’s total). Only 96 of these agencies are currently meeting APAP, meaning if the remaining 369 agencies increase their SIM or Non-SIM usage before EOY, we could theoretically close the gap to our High Confidence goal.


As noted last month, this growth is largely due to strong onboarding performance among new T10-VR customers — a result of (1) explicit T10-VR training guidelines in Axon’s version 24 Training Booklet, (2) enhanced PSO onboarding with Pro-Dev services, and (3) targeted CSM support for new Major Accounts. Starting 10/13, we are expanding this onboarding model to T1200 agencies, given its proven impact.

Looking ahead to 2026, re-engaging legacy customers will be key to increase APAP % beyond 30%. These agencies — 2+ years into their contract with little to no usage — represent one third of all APAP points. We’ve begun scheduling VOC with both inactive and lapsed customers to identify needs, and inform 2026 efforts across product features, training, and marketing.
